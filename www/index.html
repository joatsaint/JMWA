Visual Supports:
Your GPS to Navigating the
Behavioral World and Beyond

This session will focus on embedding visual supports for children and young adults with autism spectrum disorders.

Visual supports help children and young adults with ASD navigate the behavioral world by providing a concrete, visual cue that is comprehensible to the person with ASD.

These visual supports create stability and predictability in the environment and can be used to facilitate transitions, set behavioral expectations, monitor behavioral expectations and teach emotional regulation skills.

The systematic integration of visual supports is one step in providing a comprehensible environment for those affected by ASD.

Andrea D. Cherry, M.Ed., has worked with children and young adults with Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD) for more than 20 years.

She has an extensive background in Applied Behavior Analysis, structured teaching and TEACCH, PECS, sensory integration and social thinking.

She is currently the managing partner of Navigating Behavior Solutions, LLC, where she develops and implements social thinking groups for children, adolescents and young adults affected by ASD and related social nuance differences.

To Infinity and Beyond:
Social Skills for Secondary
and Post-Secondary Development

This session is designed to address the social needs of individuals at the secondary and post-secondary levels.

Topics will include:
*social boundaries including talk, touch and trust

Participants will also enjoy hands-on activities that are sure to encourage anyone to step out of their box for a little fun.

Function Junction:
Examining the Crossroads of Inappropriate Behaviors
and How to Decrease Them

This session will help you learn to identify the function of inappropriate behavior and give strategies to use to help decrease the problematic behavior.

We will examine the four functions of behavior:
escape,
attention,
automatic reinforcement, and
tangible functions.

Understanding the function of inappropriate behavior can aid:
clinicians,
teachers, and
parents in decreasing these behaviors.
